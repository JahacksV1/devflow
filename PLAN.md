# Landing Page — Project Plan

**Purpose:** One standalone page for cold outreach to local service businesses. Proves we're legit, shows what we build, makes it easy to start a conversation.

**Primary CTA (locked):** Get a free mockup of your site
**Secondary action:** View examples (in-page anchor, not a competing button)
**Audience:** Owners of roofing, tree service, landscaping, plumbing, HVAC, and similar local service businesses — mostly 35-60, mostly on phones.

---

## 1. Standards We're Building To (2026 bar)

These are non-negotiable targets. The page itself has to hit them — it IS the proof of our pitch.

### Performance
- **LCP ≤ 2.0s** on mobile (tightened from 2.5s in the March 2026 core update)
- **INP ≤ 150ms**
- **CLS ≤ 0.1**
- 75% of real-user visits must score green on all three
- Our pitch claim "we build fast modern sites" dies if this page is slow

### Mobile-first, not responsive
- 75%+ of traffic will be on phones
- Purpose-built mobile experiences win ~38% more A/B tests than scaled-down desktop
- Thumb-reachable primary CTA, sticky mobile action bar, single-column hero on phones

### Hero has 3 seconds
- Answer "who is this for / what do they get" in one sentence
- One primary CTA above the fold, not two competing ones
- Micro-proof placed directly next to the CTA, not in a separate trust section
- Real screenshots over abstract gradients (major 2023→2026 trend reversal)

### Visual language
- Bold oversized type + generous whitespace ("anti-design")
- Bento-grid modularity for mixed sections
- Raw/human photography over stock or AI imagery (trust signal against AI slop)
- 2–3 color system max, one variable font family

### Trust math (cold outreach)
- Trust = (Credibility + Value + Empathy) − Risk
- Cold visitors start at zero trust — every element either adds credibility or reduces risk
- Founder face + real name converts better than polished anonymity
- Message-matching (page reflects the industry we're pitching) is the single biggest lift

### Accessibility — WCAG 2.2 AA
- Focus Not Obscured (2.4.11) — sticky mobile bar must never cover focused elements
- Contrast ratios ≥ 4.5:1 body / 3:1 large
- Keyboard navigable, tap targets ≥ 44px
- Bonus: our audience skews 45+, so higher contrast and larger type is audience-fit anyway

### AI-readability
- Semantic HTML, clean H1/H2 hierarchy, FAQ schema
- Small-biz owners now ask ChatGPT "is this developer legit?" — the page needs to be parseable and cite-able by LLMs

## 3. Section Specs

### 3.1 Hero
- **Headline** with a rotating vertical word:
  "We rebuild outdated websites for **roofers / tree services / landscapers / plumbers / HVAC pros**..."
- **Subline:** "Built for local service businesses — designed to look clean, load fast, and turn visitors into calls."
- **Primary CTA:** `Get a free mockup of your site` (bold button, accent color)
- **Secondary:** `See examples →` (ghost text link, anchors to section 3)
- **Micro-proof line under CTA:** small meta text, e.g. "3 sites shipped · avg. 1.4s load · mobile-first"
- **Hero visual:** short looping video of a demo scrolling on a phone in a device frame (see Section 6 for sources)
- **Layout:** text left / visual right on desktop, text-over-visual single column on mobile

### 3.2 Industries Strip
- One horizontal scroll-line of industry chips immediately after hero
- **Full industry list** (use top 10-12 visible, rest available for SEO copy):
  - Roofing
  - Tree Service
  - Landscaping
  - Plumbing
  - HVAC
  - Electrical
  - Pressure Washing
  - Fencing
  - Junk Removal
  - Concrete / Masonry
  - Window Cleaning
  - Gutter Cleaning
  - Pool Service
  - Pest Control
  - Painting
  - Handyman
  - Appliance Repair
  - Garage Door
  - Locksmith
  - Moving / Hauling
  - Auto Detailing
  - Septic
  - Towing
  - Cleaning Services
- **Behavior:** on mobile, horizontal scroll with snap; on desktop, wrap or marquee
- **Purpose:** cold visitor self-identifies in under a second

### 3.3 Demo Showcase
- **3 demos at launch**, each treated as a mini case study
- **Each demo card contains:**
  - Device-framed screenshot (phone preferred, or phone + desktop)
  - Industry label
  - One-line problem: "Old site was slow and had no clear way to call"
  - One-line change: "Rebuilt with mobile-first layout and sticky call bar"
  - One-line outcome: "Loads in 1.2s · one-tap contact · clear calls to action"
- **At least one demo uses a before/after slider** — highest-converting format for redesign pitches
- **No industry filter** at 3 demos — add it when we have 6+

### 3.4 What We Build (3 Cards)
Outcome-framed titles, service description underneath. Bento layout — one larger card + two smaller, or equal thirds.

- **Card 1 — "Websites that actually load"** (Refresh & Redesign)
  We take outdated sites and rebuild them into clean, mobile-first layouts that look modern and bring in calls.
- **Card 2 — "More calls, fewer missed leads"** (Lead Capture & Contact Flow)
  Clear call buttons, quote forms, and simple layouts so customers reach you in one tap.
- **Card 3 — "24/7 tools that work while you sleep"** (Modern Add-ons)
  AI chat assistants, booking, review displays, and automated quote tools that work when you can't answer the phone.

Hover: subtle 2-4px lift, soft shadow increase. Nothing dramatic.

### 3.5 Why This Works (objection answers)
Checklist, but each line connects a pain to a result, not a feature.

- Your customers are on phones → we build phone-first
- They don't scroll forever → one tap to call you
- They bounce from slow sites → ours loads in under 2 seconds
- They don't trust ugly → clean design signals competence
- They can't find your number → we put it in reach on every screen
- They want to reach you now → forms and calls go straight to your phone

### 3.6 How It Works (process)
Four-step horizontal strip with small numbered icons.

1. **Send us your site** — or tell us your business type
2. **We build a free mockup** — you see exactly what your new site could look like
3. **You approve changes** — we revise until it's right
4. **We ship it** — live and ready in about a week

This section exists to collapse risk. Cold prospects' #1 unspoken fear is "how long, how painful, how much surprise."

### 3.7 Advanced / AI Section
Compact, positioned as "if you need more." Headline: **"Need more than a website?"**

List (concrete outcomes, not tech buzzwords):
- Chat that answers "do you service my area?" at 2am
- Instant quote estimators for standard jobs
- Automatic lead capture that texts you when someone fills a form
- Booking calendars that sync to your phone

### 3.8 FAQ
Short accordion, 5–6 questions max. Dual purpose: kill objections + FAQ schema for LLM citation.

- How much does this cost?
- How long does it take?
- What if I don't like the design?
- Do I have to switch hosts, email, or anything?
- Can you keep my current domain and content?
- Do you handle ongoing updates?

### 3.9 Contact / Final CTA
Three elements, in this order:

1. **One-field URL capture** — `Drop your website URL, we'll send you a free mockup.` Single input + single button. Highest-converting capture for this exact business model.
2. **Direct action buttons** (mobile = one tap each):
   - Call → `tel:` link, dialer opens prefilled
   - Text → `sms:` link, SMS app opens with prefilled message
   - Email → `mailto:` link with prefilled subject
3. **Founder block** — real photo, real first name, one-line honest statement. Example: "Hi, I'm [name]. I build these sites myself. Reply anytime."

### 3.10 Sticky Mobile Call Bar
Fixed bottom bar on mobile, appears after the user scrolls past the hero. Two buttons: `Call` and `Get mockup`. Respects WCAG 2.2 Focus Not Obscured — must not cover focused form fields.

### 3.11 Footer
Minimal. Business name, short contact line, copyright, one line of structured metadata. No sitemap link spam.

## 4. Visual System

| Element | Choice | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Theme | **All dark** | Premium developer-brand feel; contrasts with the light customer-facing SMB sites we're rebuilding. Revisit if it doesn't feel right. |
| Base colors | Near-black background (#0A0A0B or similar) + warm off-white text (#F4F4F1) | Not pure black on pure white — reduces harshness |
| Accent | **TBD** — leaning electric blue, warm amber, or lime. Pick one. | Used for primary CTA button, active states, single underlines |
| Typography | One variable font — Geist or Inter | Display sizes 64-96px desktop / 40-56px mobile for H1 |
| Icons | Lucide (1.5px stroke) | Consistent weight, lightweight, no FontAwesome/emoji |
| Corners | 12-16px on cards, 8px on buttons | Current, not over-rounded |
| Shadows | Soft, low-opacity, large blur, max 2 elevation levels | |
| Motion | Scroll-triggered fades, subtle hover lift, one hero animation | Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` |
| Device frames | Keep demo screenshots inside realistic phone/desktop frames | Sells the "here's what yours could look like" point |

## 5. Tech Stack

- **Framework:** Next.js 16 (App Router)
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS + shadcn/ui
- **Images:** `next/image` (prevents CLS, automatic WebP/AVIF)
- **Fonts:** `next/font` (zero layout shift)
- **Hosting:** Vercel (edge, matches our speed claim)
- **Analytics:** Vercel Analytics or Plausible — lightweight, cookieless, no consent banner needed in most regions
- **Form handler:** Edge function → Resend (email) + optional webhook to SMS/Slack
- **Content:** In-repo MDX or JSX — no CMS overhead for a single page
- **Schema:** JSON-LD for FAQ, Organization, and LocalBusiness where applicable

## 6. Media Assets — Where to Get Them

### Hero loop video (short, ~4-8 seconds, looped)
Priority order, most authentic first:

1. **Record it yourself on a real phone** — use QuickTime mirroring (Mac) or ScreenStudio to screen-record one of your three demos being scrolled. Most honest, most on-brand.
2. **ScreenStudio** or **Screen Studio** (screenstudio.com) — Mac app, zoom/pan automation, exports clean mp4
3. **Loom** → export mp4, compress with `ffmpeg` for web
4. **Rotato** (rotato.app) — adds device mockups with motion to any screen recording
5. **Mockuuups Studio** — similar, with drag-drop device frames and motion
6. **LottieFiles** — if we want a vector animation instead of video (smaller file, infinite scale)
7. **Pexels Videos** / **Coverr** / **Mixkit** — free stock video if we want generic B-roll (last resort, less authentic)

Recommended: record your actual demos on a real phone. That IS the proof.

### Section imagery (via Unsplash MCP)
The `user-unsplash` MCP is connected. We can pull images directly with `search_photos`. Use for:
- Background texture behind industries strip (abstract, low-contrast)
- Industry accent imagery if we decide to add one photo per demo
- Ambient imagery in the "How It Works" or "Advanced" section

Do NOT use Unsplash for:
- Founder photo (must be a real photo of you)
- Demo screenshots (must be your actual work)
- Client/testimonial imagery (must be real)

### Device mockup frames
- Rotato, Mockuuups Studio, or shadcn-style SVG frames for static shots
- If going vector: ShapeDivider / Figma community phone mockups

### Founder photo
- Real photo, neutral background, natural light, looking at camera or near-camera
- High-res (we'll use `next/image` to serve optimized sizes)
- One shot is fine — we only need it once in the contact section

## 8. Build Steps (in execution order)

1. **Lock brand decisions** — name, domain, accent color, final founder photo
2. **Scaffold the Next.js 16 project** — Tailwind, shadcn/ui, fonts, base dark theme
3. **Build the design tokens** — colors, spacing scale, type scale, shadow system
4. **Build section components in order:**
   1. Footer (simplest, gets structure out of the way)
   2. Sticky mobile call bar
   3. Industries strip
   4. What We Build (3 cards)
   5. Why This Works
   6. How It Works
   7. Advanced / AI
   8. FAQ (with schema)
   9. Contact block (URL form + direct actions + founder block)
   10. Demo Showcase (most content-heavy, build once content is ready)
   11. Hero (build last — needs the video asset in place)
5. **Wire the form** — edge function + Resend + webhook
6. **Add schema markup** — FAQ, Organization
7. **Record and compress the hero video** — target < 500KB, use poster image for LCP
8. **Performance pass** — Lighthouse, real-device testing, hit all Core Web Vitals targets
9. **Accessibility pass** — keyboard nav, focus order, contrast audit, `prefers-reduced-motion`
10. **Final content pass** — rewrite every word to sound human and outcome-focused
11. **Deploy to Vercel**, point domain, set up analytics
12. **Create outreach link variants** — UTM tags by industry so we can see which verticals convert

## 10. Brand Name Candidates

Positioning goal: feel **trades-adjacent, not tech-agency**. Most small-biz owners mistrust "digital studios" and "pixel labs." Our name should feel like something a contractor wouldn't be embarrassed to hire.

### Stronger direction (rugged / trades-feeling)
- **Curbline** — "curb appeal for websites"
- **Ridgework** — construction feel, works for any trade
- **Trunkline** — direct-line / tree-service double meaning
- **Mainline** — solid, plumbing-feel, memorable
- **Fieldline** — fieldwork, jobsite, direct
- **Jobline** — short, direct, blue-collar tone
- **Haulcraft** — work-truck feel with craftsmanship
- **Offhour** — "your site works off-hours" (strong tagline hook)
- **Afterhours** — same idea, slightly more brandable
- **Siteforge** — double meaning: website + jobsite
- **Bluegrip** — tactile, trades-adjacent, memorable

### Cleaner / more neutral direction
- **Callcraft** — craft + the outcome (phone calls)
- **Northworks / Ironworks / Dayworks** — -works suffix reads as workshop/trade
- **Pikedev** — short, punchy, neutral
- **Plainsite** — simple and memorable

### Recommendation to start checking
Shortlist for domain availability: **Curbline**, **Offhour**, **Trunkline**, **Ridgework**, **Siteforge**. Check `.com` first, `.co` or `.dev` as fallback.
